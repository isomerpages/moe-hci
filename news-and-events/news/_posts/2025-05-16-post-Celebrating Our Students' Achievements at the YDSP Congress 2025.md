<p>On 25 Apr 2025, our students participated in the Young Defence Scientists
Programme (YDSP) Congress, an annual event organised by the Defence Science
and Technology Agency (DSTA) to celebrate student achievements in science
and technology. This year's congress was a proud moment for our school
as several of our students were recognised for their outstanding performance
and potential in STEM.</p>

<p>Beyond receiving scholarships, several HCI students also presented their
research posters developed under the Research@YDSP programme.&nbsp;</p>
<p>A special commendation goes to Jethro Chai Tsin Kai (25S6H), who presented
his research project developed in collaboration with teammates from NUS
High School and Raffles Institution.</p>
<p>In addition, Boyce Ang Kok Hong (25S6G) and his team were selected to
share their learning experience from the YDSP World of Science, an outreach
event aimed at igniting interest in defence science. Their presentation
was attended by Senior Minister of State for Defence Mr Heng Chee How,
making the experience all the more memorable.</p>
