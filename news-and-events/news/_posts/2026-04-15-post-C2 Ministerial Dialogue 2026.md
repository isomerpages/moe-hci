<p>On 25 Mar 2026, Hwa Chong Institution had the privilege of welcoming Minister
for Foreign Affairs Dr Vivian Balakrishnan for a dialogue session with
the J2 students.</p>
<p>Against the backdrop of current geopolitical tensions and the growing
challenges facing both the world and Singapore, the dialogue with Minister
Balakrishnan gave students the rare opportunity to deepen their understanding
on pressing international developments and the foreign policy considerations
that shape Singapore’s place in an increasingly complex global landscape.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/News and Events/MD/MD01.png">
</div>
<p>The session opened with an address by Minister Balakrishnan, who spoke
about the realities of a rapidly evolving geopolitical environment marked
by rising tensions among major powers. He shared his perspectives on what
Singaporeans should expect in this new era, how Singapore was navigating
emerging global challenges in response to emerging global uncertainties,
and why it was important for young people to remain adaptable, informed,
and resilient. Minister Balakrishnan also encouraged students to think
critically and to consider how they could prepare themselves to contribute
meaningfully to Singapore. His remarks underscored the importance of staying
globally aware while remaining grounded in Singapore’s values, interests,
and long-term priorities.</p>
<p>Following the address, Minister Balakrishnan engaged students in a dialogue
session, responding to a range of questions related to international relations
and Singapore’s role on the world stage. The discussion centred on Singapore’s
response amidst recent geopolitical tensions, how the nation navigates
complex relationships between major powers, and how a small state like
Singapore maintains relevance, agency, and strategic clarity in an increasingly
uncertain world.</p>

<p>The dialogue was a valuable opportunity for students to hear directly
from the Minister for Foreign Affairs and to gain deeper insights into
the thinking behind the nation’s foreign policy. More importantly, it invited
students to reflect on the responsibilities of citizenship in an interconnected
world, and on the role they may one day play in helping Singapore navigate
the challenges and opportunities of the future.</p>
<p>Hwa Chong Institution sincerely thanks Minister Balakrishnan for taking
the time to engage our students and for sharing his valuable perspectives
with candour and generosity. His visit was greatly appreciated, and the
dialogue was a meaningful and enriching experience for all present.</p>
