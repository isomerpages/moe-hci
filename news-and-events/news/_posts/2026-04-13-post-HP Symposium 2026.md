<p>Hwa Chong Humanities Programme (HP) students, alongside students from
Art Elective Programme, Bicultural Studies Programme, Science Math Talent
Programme, Computing Talent Programme, High School section, Nanyang Girls’
High School and students from the Humanities Programmes of other junior
colleges, came together for the annual Hwa Chong Humanities Programme Symposium
on 11 Mar 2026.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/News and Events/HP/HP01.png">
</div>
<p>This year’s theme is “ Building Equitable Communities “, highlighting
how citizens of today aim to ensure greater equality for all. This can
be done via the examination of inequalities within Singaporean society
that may be overlooked, alongside aims of exploring how inequality is produced,
experienced and potentially addressed.&nbsp;</p>
<p>We were honoured to welcome back our distinguished alumni Nicolette Tan,
Issac Ong, Kimberly Chia and Wong Yang, who joined us as guest panelists,
providing eye-opening insights with the attendees.</p>

<p>Our C2 HP students also had the opportunity to present their capstone
projects, spotlighting topics from legal resources for migrant populations
to linguistic prejudice in countries such as Singapore and Taiwan. Individual
presentations by the students were also shared, with hopes of sparking
deeper thinking and debate towards the problem of equality within society.</p>
