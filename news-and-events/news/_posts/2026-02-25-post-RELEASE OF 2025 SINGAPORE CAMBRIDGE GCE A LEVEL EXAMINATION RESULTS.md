<p>Dear Student,</p>
<p><strong><em>Results Release and Collection of Official Documents</em></strong>
</p>
<p>1. &nbsp; &nbsp; The results of the 2025 Singapore-Cambridge General Certificate
of Education Advanced Level (GCE A-Level) Examination will be released
on Friday, 27 February 2026, 2:30 pm.</p>
<p>2. &nbsp; &nbsp; If you are well, you may collect your certificate and
other official documents in school on 27 February 2026. Please refer to
the instructions on the next page.</p>
<p>3. &nbsp; &nbsp; If your family would like to accompany you, please arrange
for <strong>no more than two family members</strong> to be present.</p>
<p>4. &nbsp; &nbsp; If you are unwell or unable to return to school for other
reasons, you may check your results online via Singapore Examinations and
Assessment Board’s (SEAB) Candidates Portal at<a href="http://www.seab.gov.sg/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"> </a><a href="http://www.seab.gov.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>http://www.seab.gov.sg</u></a> from 3:15
pm on 27 February 2026, till 11 pm on 13 March 2026.</p>
<p>5. &nbsp; &nbsp; If you are eligible for Singpass, you can use your Singpass
account to access SEAB's Candidates Portal. School candidates who are International
Students will have their system-generated username sent to their email
addresses which our school provided to SEAB from 25 February 2026.</p>
<p>6. &nbsp; &nbsp; Alternatively, you may authorise a proxy to collect the
official documents on your behalf. Please refer to the instructions on
the next page and use the authorisation form provided.</p>
<p><strong><em>Applications to Autonomous Universities</em></strong>
</p>
<p>7. &nbsp; &nbsp; Application for admissions and/or scholarships to the
Autonomous Universities (AUs) can be done online. Students do not need
to submit a hard copy of their GCE A-Level Certificates. More information
on the AUs' admissions processes can be found on their respective websites,
and students should approach the AUs directly for further clarifications.</p>
<p><strong><em>Education and Career Guidance (ECG) Support</em></strong>
</p>
<p>8. &nbsp; &nbsp; Students who need guidance on their education options
can approach their teachers or the ECG Counsellors supporting their schools.
They can refer to the following websites for more information:</p>
<p>· &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong>MOE's CourseFinder</strong> (<a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/coursefinder" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>https://www.moe.gov.sg/coursefinder</u></a>)</p>
<p>· &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong>MySkillsFuture Student Portal</strong> (
<a href="https://go.gov.sg/mysfpreu" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>https://go.gov.sg/mysfpreu</u>
</a>)</p>
<p>9. &nbsp; &nbsp; To complement the ECG services provided by schools, students
may make an ECG counselling appointment via the ECG Centre @ MOE at <a href="https://go.gov.sg/moe-ecg-centre" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>https://go.gov.sg/moe-ecg-centre</u></a>.
The ECG Centre @ MOE offers online or phone counselling services from Monday,
23 February 2026 to Thursday, 19 March 2026 (Weekdays: 9am to 5pm; Saturdays:
9am to 12pm).</p>
<p>10.&nbsp; We wish you all the best in your future endeavours. If you have
any questions that may not be addressed by the contacts below, please email
the school at <a href="mailto:Alevel@hci.edu.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Alevel@hci.edu.sg</a>
</p>
